Ph.D. Student · The Ohio State University

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agricultural, Environmental, and Development Economics at The Ohio State University.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and energy economics and international trade. I use forward-looking quantitative models and applied econometric methods to study climate and trade policy, the clean-energy transition, structural transformation, and household responses to environmental challenges.

I am on the 2026–2027 academic job market.

Research Fields

Environmental and Energy Economics · International Trade · Climate Economics

Methods: Dynamic Integrated Assessment Modeling · Quantitative General Equilibrium Modeling · Applied Econometrics

Job Market Paper

Dynamic Effects of Trade Policies in a Global Climate–Trade Economy

This paper develops a forward-looking, multi-region climate–trade model with bilateral trade, endogenous investment, clean and dirty energy capacity, climate policy, and climate–economy feedbacks. I use the model to study how trade policies reshape the clean-energy transition, emissions, climate outcomes, and welfare over time.
