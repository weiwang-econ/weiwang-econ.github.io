Wei Wang

Ph.D. Student · The Ohio State University

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agricultural, Environmental, and Development Economics at The Ohio State University.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and energy economics and international trade. I develop forward-looking quantitative models to study how climate and trade policies shape investment, clean-energy deployment, emissions, structural transformation, and welfare across countries.

I also use individual and household survey data, applied econometric methods, and discrete-choice experiments to study nonmarket valuation, behavioral responses to environmental risks, and household decision-making related to sustainable resource use.

I am on the 2026–2027 academic job market.

Research Fields

Environmental and Energy Economics · Climate Economics · International Trade

Methods: Dynamic Integrated Assessment Modeling · Quantitative General Equilibrium Modeling · Applied Econometrics · Discrete Choice Experiments
