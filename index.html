I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agricultural, Environmental, and Development Economics at The Ohio State University. My advisor is Yongyang Cai .

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and energy economics, international trade, and climate economics. I develop forward-looking quantitative models to study how climate and trade policies shape investment, clean-energy deployment, emissions, structural transformation, and welfare across countries.

I am on the 2026–2027 academic job market.

Research

Dynamic Effects of Trade Policies in a Global Climate–Trade Economy

Job Market Paper

This paper develops a forward-looking, multi-region climate–trade model with bilateral trade, endogenous investment, clean and dirty energy capacity, climate policy, and climate–economy feedbacks. I use the model to study how trade policies reshape the clean-energy transition, emissions, climate outcomes, and welfare over time.

Draft and updated abstract coming soon.

Working Papers

Rising Temperatures, Structural Transformation, and Within-Country Wage Inequality

This paper examines how rising temperatures affect the skill premium through changes in sectoral employment and structural transformation. It combines individual-level panel data from China with county-level meteorological data.

Throw a Bunch Away: A Bundled Utility Maximization Framework Modeling Multiple-Item Food Discarding

With Wuyang Hu, Xiaolei Li, Jian Li, and Ping Qing.

This paper develops and empirically tests a bundled utility maximization framework for understanding interdependent household decisions to discard multiple near-expiration food items.

Work in Progress

Non-Cooperative Climate Policy in a Dynamic Trade IAM

With Yongyang Cai.

This project develops an algorithm for solving non-cooperative climate policies in dynamic, multi-region integrated assessment models with bilateral trade and forward-looking investment.

Capital Flows and Optimal Climate Policies

With Jingke Wu, Xufeng Liu, and Yongyang Cai.

This project studies how international capital flows, clean and dirty energy investment, and technology spillovers affect optimal climate policy across developed and developing regions.
