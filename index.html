I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agricultural, Environmental, and Development Economics at The Ohio State University, advised by Yongyang Cai .

I study the dynamic interactions among climate policy, international trade, investment, and the clean-energy transition. My research develops forward-looking quantitative models to evaluate how climate and trade policies affect emissions, structural transformation, and welfare across countries.

I am on the 2026–2027 academic job market.

Research Fields

Job Market Paper

Dynamic Effects of Trade Policies in a Global Climate–Trade Economy

This paper develops a forward-looking, multi-region climate–trade model with bilateral trade, endogenous investment, clean and dirty energy capacity, climate policy, and climate–economy feedbacks. I use the model to study how trade policies reshape clean-energy deployment, emissions, climate outcomes, and welfare over time.

Working Papers

Rising Temperatures, Structural Transformation, and Within-Country Wage Inequality

This paper studies how rising temperatures affect the skill premium through changes in sectoral employment and structural transformation.

Throw a Bunch Away: A Bundled Utility Maximization Framework Modeling Multiple-Item Food Discarding

With Wuyang Hu, Xiaolei Li, Jian Li, and Ping Qing. The paper develops and tests a framework for understanding interdependent household decisions to discard multiple near-expiration food items.
